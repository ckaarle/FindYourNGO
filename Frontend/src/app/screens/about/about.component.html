manage_search What is our goal?

We want to provide a user-friendly, easy-to-use way to discover NGOs that match your interests and/or your location. Our main focus is to also offer the user an approximation of how trustworthy the NGOs listed in our database are. For information on how this trustworthiness score is calculated, please see below.

In addition, we strive to provide our users with a platform where they can connect and share their experiences with the NGOs listed in out database. This includes rating an NGOs trustworthiness and adding comments.

folder_open What are our data sources?

Currently, we have access to three sources compiling lists of NGOs:

  • Conference of INGOs of the Council of Europe
  • NgoAdvisor, a commercial NGO database
  • Wango, the World Association of Non-Governmental Organizations

Among these three sources, we consider only the first one (the Council of Europe) to be absolutely trustworthy. Combined, we have over 50.000 NGOs in our database.

star How is the trustworthiness score calculated?

There are five factors included in the calculation of the trustworthiness score, resulting in a value between 0 and 5:

  • Credibility of the data source
  • Accreditation with one of the following organizations:
    • United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)
    • International Labour Organization (ILO)
    • Commonwealth Foundation
  • Adherence to the Wango Code of Ethics
  • Connections to other NGOs
  • User Ratings
